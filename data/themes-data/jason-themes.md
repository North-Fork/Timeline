## Intellectual Portrait

### The Central Axis: Indigenous Futures × Digital Technology

Jason's work sits at the intersection of two major poles that run through virtually every part of his career:

- **Indigenous futures / Indigenous digital sovereignty / Aboriginal territories in cyberspace** — not just topics but a political and artistic program: imagining, building, and defending Indigenous presence in digital space.
- **Computational media / Electronic literature / Computational poetry** — the technical and literary practice side, foregrounded in PoEMM, in his teaching, and in the journal/book output.

These two poles are rarely separate. The research-creation practice uses the tools of computational media *in the service of* Indigenous futures.

### The Five Threads

**1. Electronic Literature & Computational Poetry**
The most technically distinctive thread. PoEMM is the centrepiece — interactive, touch-based poetry built in custom Java/Objective-C. Key concepts: dynamic text, typography, iOS development, touchscreen interaction. Surfaces heavily in Creative Works, Solo/Group Exhibitions, Journal Articles, Academic Reviews.

**2. Indigenous Digital Sovereignty & Futurism**
The dominant intellectual framework. Spans funded research (PI and Co-I), keynotes, invited lectures, policy papers, and op-eds. The "University Research Chair in Computational Media and the Indigenous Future Imaginary" names this thread explicitly. Related concepts: future imaginaries, Indigenous protocols, Indigenous AI, Abundant Intelligences.

**3. Game Design as Cultural Pedagogy**
A significant parallel strand: the Skins Summer Institute, TimeTraveller game, and related workshops. Shows up strongly in Productions, Conference Presentations, PhD supervision, and Documentary screenings. Game design here is explicitly framed as Indigenous storytelling and knowledge transmission.

**4. AI Ethics & Indigenous AI**
A newer but rapidly growing thread (strong in Keynotes, Policy Papers, Invited Lectures, Op-Eds, Books/Chapters). Concepts include Abundant Intelligences, Indigenous AI, AI ethics. Collaborator Suzanne Kite is central here.

**5. Curatorial Practice & Community Building**
Running underneath everything: organizing residencies, symposia, exhibitions, and lecture series. Community engagement is #6 by frequency. AbTeC itself is as much a curatorial/community project as a research lab.
